## What was actually at stake

## What I pushed back on

Did the brief, the stakeholders, or the process ask for something you disagreed with? What did you argue for instead, and how did that resolve?

If nothing was pushed back on, either the project was unusually well-framed or this section needs more honesty.

---

## Outcome

What shipped. What changed. What was measured if anything was measured.

Keep this proportional to confidence — don't attribute business outcomes to design decisions you can't directly trace.

---

## What I'd do differently

This is the section that separates designers who reflect from designers who perform. One or two things, specific, honest.

Not "I would have done more user research" — that's a placeholder answer. What specific decision, made differently, would have changed the quality of the outcome?
